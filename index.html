English Text Types

1. Notices

Notices give clear instructions, rules, or prohibitions in public places.

Example:

  • “Do not litter in this area.”
  • “No food and drink allowed.”

Types include:

  • Prohibition: “No Smoking”, “Do Not Enter”
  • Warning: “Caution! Wet Floor”
  • Mandatory: “Wear a Helmet”

Example:

Sign: No swimming

Meaning: You are not allowed to swim in that area.

2. Announcements

Announcements are formal or informal messages that inform people about something important.

Purpose

The purpose is to give clear and concise information to a group of people so they know what is happening or what they need to do.

Language Features

  • Use of present tense
  • Clear and direct statements
  • Use of time expressions (e.g., tomorrow, next week)
  • Includes imperative sentences (e.g., “Please attend”, “Don’t forget”)
  • Polite and formal language

Structure

  • Opening: Getting attention (e.g., “Attention students”)
  • Content: Main message (what, when, where, who, how)
  • Closing: Reminder or polite closing (e.g., “See you there”)

3. Narrative Texts (Folklore and Fables)

Used to entertain readers and convey moral lessons.

Structure includes:

  • Orientation: Introduction of characters and setting
  • Complication: Problem arises
  • Resolution: Problem is solved

Language Features

  • Past tense verbs
  • Direct speech
  • Descriptive adjectives

Example: “Once upon a time, an elephant wandered in the forest to find friends…”

4. Recount Texts

Recounts retell personal experiences that happened in the past.

Structure:

  • Orientation: Who, when, where
  • Events: What happened
  • Reorientation: Personal comment or conclusion

Language Features

  • Use of past tense (went, visited, enjoyed)
  • Time sequence words: first, then, after that, finally

Example: “Last weekend, my family went to the zoo. We saw lions and fed giraffes. It was fun!”
